Capital Solution

Senior Debt

First-mortgage facilities for acquisition, pre-development, construction, and investment lending. Senior debt forms the backbone of most development capital structures.

Eastbridge Capital accesses a broad panel of bank and non-bank senior lenders, ensuring competitive pricing and certainty of execution across a range of asset classes and transaction sizes.

Acquisition finance against established or development sites
Pre-development and feasibility facilities
Construction facilities — residential, mixed-use, commercial
Investment lending against completed stock

02

Capital Solution

Stretched Senior Debt

Higher-leverage senior structures that extend beyond traditional LVR thresholds while maintaining senior security. Designed for sponsors seeking to maximise debt coverage and reduce equity requirements.

Typically structured with specialist non-bank lenders or through blended facilities, stretched senior debt sits in the 65%–80% LTC range depending on the asset and sponsor profile.

LVR up to 75–80% for qualifying transactions
Non-bank lender relationships for speed and flexibility
Suitable for well-located residential and mixed-use

03

Capital Solution

Mezzanine Finance

Second mortgage or subordinated debt used to reduce equity requirements, bridge funding gaps, and optimise project feasibility. Mezzanine finance sits between senior debt and equity in the capital stack.

Eastbridge Capital works with a network of specialist mezzanine funds and private lenders to structure cost-effective junior debt that complements the senior facility.

Blended to 80–90% LTC when combined with senior debt
Interest typically capitalised or paid from project proceeds
Can be structured as intercreditor or standalone

04

Capital Solution

Preferred Equity

Structured equity capital positioned between debt and common equity, providing funding while preserving sponsor control. Preferred equity investors receive priority returns before common equity participates in profit.

A sophisticated instrument used by experienced developers to fill the gap between available debt and required equity — without diluting the project's economics beyond what is required.

Structured return profile with upside participation
Preserves sponsor's majority equity position
Suitable for large-scale residential and mixed-use projects
